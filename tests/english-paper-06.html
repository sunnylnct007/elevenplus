1Sam pressed his forehead against the cold car window and watched the unfamiliar streets slide past. Everything about the new town felt wrong: the houses stood too close together, the sky seemed a duller shade of grey, and 3not a single face belonged to anyone he knew. In three days he would walk through the gates of Ashcroft Primary, a school where he was certain he would be entirely, hopelessly alone.
5“You’ll make friends in no time,” his mother promised, catching his eye in the mirror. Sam said nothing. He had heard those words before, and he did not believe them. 7Friends, he thought gloomily, were not something you could order like furniture from a catalogue.
9Monday arrived far too quickly. Sam’s stomach churned as he stepped into the noisy classroom, clutching his rucksack straps as though they might save him. 11Thirty strangers turned to stare. The teacher, Mrs Okafor, gestured towards an empty seat beside a boy with wild curls and ink-stained fingers. Sam slid into the chair, staring hard at the desk, willing himself to disappear.
14For most of the morning he barely moved. Then, during the quiet reading hour, the curly-haired boy leaned over. “Is that a comic?” he whispered, nodding at a battered notebook poking from Sam’s bag. 16Sam froze. Inside were his own drawings — a masked hero called Nighthawk that he had never shown anyone.
19“Um… sort of,” Sam mumbled, sliding the notebook further into his bag. But the boy’s eyes were already shining. 21“I draw too! I’ve got about a hundred pages of mine at home. What’s yours about?” Before Sam could stop himself, he had opened the notebook and was explaining Nighthawk’s secret origin. 23The boy, whose name was Ravi, listened as though it were the most fascinating story in the world.
25By lunchtime the two of them were huddled in a corner of the playground, swapping ideas for impossible villains and arguing cheerfully about which superpower was best. 27Sam had forgotten, entirely, that he had ever dreaded this day. Perhaps, he thought as Ravi sketched a dragon across the back of his hand, the new town was not so grey after all.
