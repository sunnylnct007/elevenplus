The Unexpected Discovery

An original passage
1Theo had always considered science club the most tedious option available. When Mrs Harding announced that the after-school club had exactly one remaining space, Theo had stared fixedly at his desk, willing himself invisible. Yet somehow — he still wasn’t entirely sure how — he had found himself writing 5his name on the sign-up sheet.
The following Tuesday, clutching his notebook and trying to look as though he belonged, Theo trudged alongside five other pupils down the muddy path that led to Crestfield Wood. The science teacher, Mr Osei, walked ahead with long, enthusiastic strides. “Keep your eyes open,” Mr Osei called cheerfully over his shoulder. 10“Nature reveals herself only to those who look.”
Theo rolled his eyes. He had expected experiments with beakers and Bunsen burners, not a march through soggy leaves. But then something odd happened. As they rounded a bend in the path, a flash of vivid colour caught his eye — something dancing between two oak trees in a patch of pale autumn sunlight. He stopped walking.
15Cautiously, barely breathing, Theo stepped off the path. The creature settled on a bramble, its wings fanning open to reveal a pattern so intricate that it seemed almost painted. Two deep-purple eyespots blazed against a background of burnt orange and ivory. He had never seen anything like it.
“Mr Osei,” Theo called, his voice barely above a whisper. “I think you need to come and look at this.” 20The teacher crouched beside him, and for a long moment neither of them spoke. “That,” said Mr Osei at last, his own voice hushed with wonder, “is a Purple Emperor. They are incredibly rare in lowland woodland.”
Theo’s heart hammered. He took three careful photographs with the school tablet, hands trembling. Afterwards, when Mr Osei told the group that Theo’s discovery would be reported to the county wildlife trust, Theo felt something he had never expected to feel in science club: 25pure, exhilarating pride.
