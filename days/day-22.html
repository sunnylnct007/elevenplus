1. Core skills warm-up

  1. What is 3⁵?
    243. 3×3=9, 9×3=27, 27×3=81, 81×3=243.
  2. What percentage is 45 out of 60?
    75%. (45 ÷ 60) × 100 = 75%.
  3. Solve: (x/3) + 4 = 9.
    x = 15. Subtract 4: x/3 = 5. Multiply by 3: x = 15.
  4. What is the sum of interior angles of a heptagon (7 sides)?
    900°. (7−2) × 180° = 5 × 180° = 900°.
  5. A line has equation y = −2x + 5. What is its gradient and y-intercept?
    Gradient = −2. y-intercept = 5 (where x = 0).

2. Word problems & exam-style practice

  1. Two numbers are in ratio 7:3. Their sum is 150. What are the numbers?
    105 and 45. Total parts = 10. One part = 150 ÷ 10 = 15. Larger: 7 × 15 = 105. Smaller: 3 × 15 = 45.
  2. A car travels at 90 km/h. How far does it travel in 40 minutes?
    60 km. 40 min = 2/3 hour. Distance = 90 × 2/3 = 60 km.
  3. A square has perimeter 52 cm. What is its area?
    169 cm². Side = 52 ÷ 4 = 13 cm. Area = 13² = 169 cm².
  4. In a class: 12 pupils walk, 9 cycle, 6 go by car, 3 by bus. What fraction walk?
    2/5. Total = 30. Walk = 12. Fraction = 12/30 = 2/5.
  5. A rectangular pool (25 m × 12 m) is tiled with 0.5 m × 0.5 m tiles. How many tiles are needed?
    1,200 tiles. Pool area = 300 m². Tile area = 0.25 m². 300 ÷ 0.25 = 1,200.

📖 English (~20 minutes)

1. SPaG & vocabulary

  1. What is anaphora? Give an example from a speech or poem.
    Anaphora = deliberate repetition of words at the start of successive clauses. Example (Churchill): "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields." Effect: builds rhythmic momentum, emphasis, and emotional power.
  2. Identify and correct both errors: The team are playing there best game of the season, despite loosing there last match.
    The team are playing their best game of the season, despite losing their last match. Errors: "there" → "their" (twice); "loosing" → "losing."
  3. What is the difference between a simile and a metaphor? Which is generally more powerful and why?
    Simile compares using "like" or "as" — "He was like a lion." Metaphor states one thing IS another — "He was a lion." Metaphors are often more powerful because they assert identity rather than comparison — no hedging, a collapsed distance.
  4. What does languor mean? Use it in a sentence.
    A pleasant tiredness or lack of energy, often associated with heat or relaxation. Example: "The heat of the afternoon filled the garden with a drowsy languor, and nobody moved."
  5. What is a volta in a sonnet, and why is it important?
    A volta ("turn" in Italian) is the shift in argument, mood, or perspective — typically at line 9 in a Petrarchan sonnet, or the final couplet in a Shakespearean sonnet. The octave sets up the problem; the volta introduces the resolution or twist.

2. Comprehension — Priya and the Violin

The problem with being good at something is that people stop asking if you enjoy it. By the time Priya was fourteen, she had been playing violin for nine years, and she was, by any measurable standard, exceptional. Her teachers spoke of her "gift" as though it were a thing that had happened to her, like weather. Her parents had arranged her schedule around the violin since she was five. She played at competitions, at school concerts, at family gatherings where relatives she barely knew applauded and called her remarkable. She had not been asked, in any serious way, whether she wanted to continue, because the answer seemed obvious. The violin was who she was. It was only when she broke her wrist in January and could not play for three months that she discovered, to her own surprise, that she felt something close to relief.
  1. What is Priya's situation, and how long has she been playing?
    She is fourteen years old, has been playing violin for nine years, and is considered exceptionally gifted — performing regularly at competitions, concerts, and family gatherings.
  2. What does "her teachers spoke of her gift as though it were a thing that had happened to her, like weather" suggest?
    The simile "like weather" implies her gift was beyond her control — it simply arrived. It suggests passivity: she has been shaped by her talent rather than being the agent of it. The metaphor strips her of ownership of her own ability.
  3. Why had nobody asked Priya if she wanted to continue?
    Because the answer seemed obvious — the violin was seen as inseparable from who she was. Her identity had been subsumed by her talent, so the question was never asked.
  4. What is the significance of the broken wrist and her feeling of relief?
    The injury removes external pressure, and in that space Priya discovers relief — not grief. This suggests she may not have been playing from genuine desire but from expectation and identity imposed by others.
  5. What wider issue does the writer explore through Priya's story?
    The pressure placed on talented young people; the difference between excellence and choice; how identity can be constructed around achievement rather than personal desire. The writer questions whether talent is always a gift.

3. Writing task

Write a short piece from Priya's perspective during the three months when she cannot play. What does she notice, discover, or feel? Use: interior monologue, at least one surprising detail about what she does with the freed time, and a final line that leaves her future ambiguous.

The silence in the house was the first thing she noticed. Strange, how full a house could sound when a violin was not being practised in it. She had not realised how much of her time had been filled — not with music exactly, but with obligation. Now she read books that had nothing to do with anything. She went for walks without a purpose. She sat in the garden one afternoon and watched a bee visit the same lavender stalk seventeen times. She counted. She had never counted anything that didn't matter before. Her wrist healed in March. The violin case was where she had left it, on the chair by the window, waiting. She looked at it for a long time. Then she went downstairs and made tea.

⭐ Bonus Maths Challenge

  1. Ratio & proportion: Paint colours are mixed in ratio 3:5 (red:blue). How much red is needed to make 24 litres of the mixture?
    9 litres. Total parts = 8. Red = 3/8 of 24 = 9 litres. Blue = 5/8 of 24 = 15 litres.
  2. Algebra: y = −2x + 5. Find y when x = −3, and find x when y = 11.
    When x = −3: y = −2(−3)+5 = 6+5 = 11. When y = 11: 11 = −2x+5 → −2x = 6 → x = −3. (These are the same point!)
  3. Speed: A cyclist travels at 18 km/h for 40 minutes, then 24 km/h for 30 minutes. What is the total distance?
    24 km. First leg: 18 × (40/60) = 18 × 2/3 = 12 km. Second leg: 24 × (30/60) = 24 × 1/2 = 12 km. Total = 24 km.

💬 Word of the Day

prodigy
noun
A person, especially a young one, endowed with exceptional qualities or abilities. From Latin prodigium — an omen or remarkable thing.
"Labelled a musical prodigy from the age of five, Priya had grown up feeling less like a person than a performance."
