Maths

~20 minutes
Part 1 — Core Skills Warm-up

Answer these quickly — mentally or with brief working:

  1. What is 9 × 7?
    Answer: 63
    Think of 10 × 7 = 70, then subtract 7: 70 − 7 = 63.
  2. What is 72 ÷ 8?
    Answer: 9
    Because 8 × 9 = 72, so 72 ÷ 8 = 9.
  3. Round 7,842 to the nearest thousand.
    Answer: 8,000
    The hundreds digit is 8 (≥5), so round the thousands digit up: 7,000 → 8,000.
  4. Find ⅗ of 40.
    Answer: 24
    Find ⅕ first: 40 ÷ 5 = 8. Then multiply by 3: 8 × 3 = 24.
  5. Find 25% of 180.
    Answer: 45
    25% = ¼, so 180 ÷ 4 = 45.
Part 2 — Word Problems & Exam-Style Practice

Show clear working for each question:

  1. A baker makes 144 rolls and packs them into bags of 12. How many bags does she fill?
    Answer: 12 bags
    Total rolls ÷ bag size: 144 ÷ 12 = 12.
  2. A cinema has 5 rows of seats. Each row has 18 seats. On Monday, 62 seats are empty. How many seats are taken?
    Answer: 28 seats
    Total = 5 × 18 = 90. Seats taken = 90 − 62 = 28.
  3. Mia saves £4.75 each week. How much will she have saved after 8 weeks?
    Answer: £38.00
    4.75 × 8 = (4 × 8) + (0.75 × 8) = 32 + 6 = £38.
  4. A swimming lesson starts at 14:45 and lasts 50 minutes. What time does it finish?
    Answer: 15:35
    14:45 + 15 min = 15:00, then + 35 min = 15:35.
  5. In a bag of 60 counters, ⅔ are red. How many counters are not red?
    Answer: 20 counters
    ⅓ are not red. Find ⅓ of 60: 60 ÷ 3 = 20.

English

~20–25 minutes
Part 1 — SPaG & Vocabulary
  1. Choose the correct spelling:
    A) definately    B) definatly    C) definitely
    Answer: C) definitely
    There is no ‘a’ in definitely — think of it as de-FINITE-ly (it contains the word “finite”).
  2. Add the missing punctuation:
    yesterday lucy baked a chocolate cake her sister maya ate three slices
    Corrected: Yesterday, Lucy baked a chocolate cake. Her sister Maya ate three slices.
    Capital letters for sentence starts and proper nouns (Lucy, Maya); full stop between sentences; comma after “Yesterday”.
  3. Identify the adjective in this sentence:
    The enormous dog barked at the postman.
    Answer: enormous
    An adjective is a describing word — here it tells us more about the noun “dog”.
  4. Choose a synonym for exhausted:
    A) energetic    B) tired    C) frightened
    Answer: B) tired
    A synonym has a similar meaning. Exhausted means very tired.
  5. Choose an antonym for ancient:
    A) old    B) crumbling    C) modern
    Answer: C) modern
    An antonym has the opposite meaning. Ancient means very old, so modern (new) is its antonym.
Part 2 — Comprehension

Read this passage carefully, then answer the questions:

Priya pressed her face against the cold glass of the aquarium tank. Enormous shadows drifted silently past — rays, as wide as dinner tables, gliding just centimetres away. The guide had told the class that rays could live for up to 25 years and that some species were endangered because of overfishing. Priya had always loved the sea, but today she felt something stronger than love: a fierce, quiet determination to protect it.
  1. Where is Priya?
    Answer: At an aquarium.
    She is pressing her face against the glass of an aquarium tank.
  2. What are the enormous shadows that drift past?
    Answer: Rays (a type of sea creature).
    The passage describes them as rays as wide as dinner tables.
  3. Give one reason why some rays are endangered.
    Answer: Overfishing.
    The guide tells the class that some species are endangered because of overfishing.
  4. Find and copy a simile from the passage.
    Answer: “as wide as dinner tables”
    A simile compares two things using “as” or “like”.
  5. How has visiting the aquarium changed how Priya feels about the sea? Use evidence from the passage.
    Example answer: She already loved the sea, but seeing the rays in danger made her feel a “fierce, quiet determination to protect it” — showing she now wants to take action, not just enjoy it.
Part 3 — Writing Task
Prompt: You press your hand against the cold glass of the aquarium tank. Something on the other side looks back at you. Describe what you see, how it makes you feel, and what happens next.

Write 8–10 sentences. Try to use:
  • Full stops and commas correctly
  • At least two examples of figurative language (simile, metaphor, or personification)
  • One sentence of speech (with speech marks)
Example answer:
The water was ink-dark except for a single beam of blue light. I pressed my palm flat against the glass and felt the cold shoot up my arm like an electric shock. A vast octopus emerged from the shadows, its eyes — golden and intelligent — fixing on mine as if it could read my thoughts. “It’s looking right at me,” I whispered to my teacher. The creature raised one curling arm and pressed it against the glass, almost touching my hand. I stood completely still, my heart hammering, unable to look away.

Check your own writing for: clear sentences with full stops and capital letters, correct commas, figurative language (simile, metaphor or personification), and speech marks around spoken words.

Bonus Maths Challenge

~5 minutes · optional
Harder Problems — Push Yourself!

These questions are trickier — great for extra exam practice. Take your time and show all working.

  1. A train travels 270 km in 3 hours. At the same speed, how far will it travel in 5 hours?
    Answer: 450 km
    Step 1 — find the speed: 270 ÷ 3 = 90 km/h.
    Step 2 — multiply by 5: 90 × 5 = 450 km.
  2. A rectangle has a perimeter of 36 cm. Its length is twice its width. What is the area of the rectangle?
    Answer: 72 cm²
    Let width = w, so length = 2w.
    Perimeter: 2(w + 2w) = 36 → 2(3w) = 36 → 6w = 36 → w = 6 cm.
    Length = 12 cm. Area = 12 × 6 = 72 cm².
  3. In a survey, 45 out of 180 children said their favourite colour is blue. What percentage is this?
    Answer: 25%
    45 ÷ 180 = ¼ = 25%.  Or: (45 ÷ 180) × 100 = 25.

Word of the Day

~2 minutes
tenacious adjective
ten-AY-shuss
Definition: holding firmly to something; not giving up easily; determined and persistent.
“Despite falling twice, the tenacious young climber reached the top of the wall.”
Your turn: Write your own sentence using tenacious that is set somewhere other than a sports context — try school, nature, or an adventure story!

Tip: Knowing words like tenacious helps in both comprehension questions and your own writing — examiners reward varied vocabulary!
