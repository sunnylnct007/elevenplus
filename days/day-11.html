Day 11

LCM, Standard Form & Mr Okafor’s Allotment

Maths · English · Bonus Challenge · Word of the Day

🕑 Maths: 20 min 🕑 English: 20 min ★ Bonus: 10 min
✎

Maths (about 20 minutes)

1. Core skills warm-up

  1. What is 19 × 7?
    Answer: 133. Use (20 × 7) − (1 × 7) = 140 − 7 = 133.
  2. What is the LCM of 6 and 9?
    Answer: 18. Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18… Multiples of 9: 9, 18… First common multiple = 18.
  3. What is 3/4 − 1/6? Give your answer in simplest form.
    Answer: 7/12. Common denominator = 12. 3/4 = 9/12, 1/6 = 2/12. 9/12 − 2/12 = 7/12.
  4. Write 520,000 in standard form.
    Answer: 5.2 × 10&sup5;. Move the decimal 5 places left: 5.2 × 100,000.
  5. What is the range of: 4, 17, 9, 3, 22, 11?
    Answer: 19. Highest − lowest = 22 − 3 = 19.

2. Word problems & exam-style practice

  1. A school raises £2,340 for charity, split equally among 9 charities. How much per charity?
    Answer: £260. 2,340 ÷ 9 = 260.
  2. A rectangle has width 9 cm and area 108 cm². Find the length and perimeter.
    Length = 108 ÷ 9 = 12 cm. Perimeter = 2 × (12 + 9) = 42 cm.
  3. A car uses 1 litre per 12 km. How many litres are needed for 210 km?
    Answer: 17.5 litres. 210 ÷ 12 = 17.5.
  4. Scores: 55, 62, 71, 68, 44, 80, 55, 73. Find the mode and median.
    Mode = 55 (appears twice). Ordered: 44, 55, 55, 62, 68, 71, 73, 80. Median = (62 + 68) ÷ 2 = 65.
  5. A 4.8 m plank is cut into 60 cm pieces. How many pieces? Any left over?
    Convert: 4.8 m = 480 cm. 480 ÷ 60 = 8 pieces exactly. No remainder.

English (about 20 minutes)

1. SPaG & vocabulary

  1. Identify the conjunction and state if it is coordinating or subordinating:
    She practised every day because she wanted to improve.
    “Because” is a subordinating conjunction. It joins a main clause to a subordinate clause that depends on it. (Coordinating conjunctions join equal clauses: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so.)
  2. Punctuate correctly:
    the museum said the guide is home to over 3000 artefacts including items from ancient egypt greece and rome
    The museum, said the guide, is home to over 3,000 artefacts, including items from ancient Egypt, Greece, and Rome. Capital for proper nouns (Egypt, Greece, Rome); comma around reporting clause; comma before list; number written with comma.
  3. Give synonyms and antonyms for the word brave.
    Synonyms: courageous, fearless, bold, valiant, intrepid. Antonyms: cowardly, timid, fearful, craven.
  4. What does ominous mean? Use it in a sentence about weather.
    Ominous means giving a warning that something bad is about to happen — threatening. Example: “An ominous bank of dark clouds gathered on the horizon.”
  5. What is an ellipsis (…)? Give an example used for suspense in fiction.
    An ellipsis is three dots showing a trailing off, a pause, or omission. In fiction it creates suspense or uncertainty. Example: “He opened the box. Inside was something he had never expected to find…”

2. Short comprehension

Every morning, without fail, Mr Okafor arrived at the allotment before anyone else. He was there when the mist still lay across the vegetable beds, when the gate creaked as it swung open, when the world was still half-asleep. He never seemed to hurry. He moved between the rows of beans and cabbages with a slow, considered care — touching a leaf here, crouching to inspect the soil there — as if he were conducting a quiet conversation with each plant in turn. The other allotment holders respected him enormously, though few of them could have explained why. Perhaps it was simply that he treated the earth as if it mattered.
  1. When does Mr Okafor arrive?
    Before anyone else — when the mist still lies across the beds and the world is “still half-asleep.”
  2. What does “conducting a quiet conversation with each plant” suggest about him?
    This metaphor suggests he tends each plant individually with great attentiveness, as though each has its own needs — implying a deep, personal relationship with his garden and extraordinary patience.
  3. Why do others respect him, even though they can’t explain it?
    They can see the care and patience he brings to his work. The final line gives the clue: “he treated the earth as if it mattered” — a quiet dignity others feel but cannot name.
  4. How does the writer use listing (anaphora) in this passage? What is the effect?
    The repeated “when” clauses build a vivid, layered picture of the early morning ritual, creating a rhythmic, almost incantatory quality — reinforcing how regular and devoted his visits are.
  5. What overall impression of Mr Okafor does the writer create? How?
    Calm, wise, meditative — achieved through his slow movements, the plant-conversation metaphor, and the understated final sentence: simple, quiet, and profound.

3. Short writing task

Write a short piece (about 8–10 sentences):

“Describe a person completely devoted to one thing — a hobby, craft, or place. Capture what makes them special without stating it directly.”

Try to use: show not tell, a metaphor for how they interact with their passion, and a quiet final sentence.

Example answer (a baker):
My grandfather gets up at four o’clock and nobody has ever asked him to. By the time we wake, the table is covered in flour like a light fall of snow. He doesn’t use recipes — he says the dough tells you what it needs, if you listen. When he kneads the bread, his hands work like a conductor leading an orchestra without looking at the score. Every morning the evidence is on the cooling rack. It is enough.

Check for: (1) show not tell: no sentence says “he is passionate” — we infer it (2) metaphor: “hands work like a conductor” (3) quiet final sentence: “It is enough.”

Bonus Maths Challenge

Three harder problems to stretch your thinking.

  1. Standard form: Write 0.00047 in standard form. Then calculate (3 × 10&sup4;) × (2 × 10³) and give your answer in standard form.
    0.00047 = 4.7 × 10−4. (3 × 10&sup4;) × (2 × 10³) = 6 × 10&sup7; = 60,000,000.
  2. Mode, median, mean puzzle: A data set of 5 numbers has a mean of 10, a median of 9, and a mode of 8. Two of the numbers are 8 and 8. Find the other three numbers.
    Total = 5 × 10 = 50. Known: 8 + 8 = 16. Remaining three sum to 34. Median (middle value when ordered) = 9, so the third number is 9. Remaining two sum to 25 and must both be ≥ 9. Possible: 8, 8, 9, ?, ? where ?+? = 25. E.g. 12 and 13 (or 11 and 14). Many correct answers — the key constraints are: two 8s, middle value = 9, total = 50.
  3. LCM problem: A red light flashes every 6 seconds, a blue light every 8 seconds, and a green light every 9 seconds. They all flash together at time zero. After how many seconds do they next all flash together?
    Find LCM(6, 8, 9). Prime factors: 6=2×3, 8=2³, 9=3². LCM = 2³ × 3² = 8 × 9 = 72 seconds.

Word of the Day

meticulous
/ meh-TIK-yoo-lus /  —  adjective
Showing great attention to detail and care; very precise and thorough. A meticulous person checks every small thing and leaves nothing to chance.
“Mr Okafor’s meticulous attention to each plant — touching a leaf here, inspecting the soil there — was what set him apart from the other allotment holders.”
