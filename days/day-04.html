Maths

~20 minutes
Part 1 — Core Skills Warm-up

Answer these quickly — mentally or with brief working:

  1. What is 7 × 13?
    Answer: 91
    Split it: (7 × 10) + (7 × 3) = 70 + 21 = 91.
  2. What is 156 ÷ 12?
    Answer: 13
    Check: (12 × 10) + (12 × 3) = 120 + 36 = 156. ✓
  3. List all the factors of 36.
    Answer: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36
    Work in pairs: 1×36, 2×18, 3×12, 4×9, 6×6. Stop when the pairs meet.
    1 2 3 4 6 9 12 18 36
  4. What is 40% of 85?
    Answer: 34
    10% of 85 = 8.5.  40% = 8.5 × 4 = 34.
  5. What are the next two terms in this sequence?  100, 91, 82, 73, …
    Answer: 64 and 55
    Rule: subtract 9 each time.  73 − 9 = 64,  64 − 9 = 55.
Part 2 — Word Problems & Exam-Style Practice

Show clear working for each question:

  1. A school library has 840 books. 35% are non-fiction. How many non-fiction books are there?
    Answer: 294 books
    10% of 840 = 84.  30% = 84 × 3 = 252.  5% = 84 ÷ 2 = 42.  35% = 252 + 42 = 294.
  2. Ahmed thinks of a number, doubles it, then subtracts 7. The answer is 19. What was his number?
    Answer: 13
    Work backwards using inverse operations:
    19 + 7 = 26,  then 26 ÷ 2 = 13.  Check: 13 × 2 − 7 = 19. ✓
    ?
    ×2 →
    ×2
    −7 →
    19
    reverse: +7 then ÷2
  3. A shop sells apples for 35p each and bananas for 22p each. Fiona buys 4 apples and 3 bananas. How much change does she get from £3.00?
    Answer: 94p
    Apples: 4 × 35p = 140p.  Bananas: 3 × 22p = 66p.
    Total spent: 140 + 66 = 206p = £2.06.
    Change: £3.00 − £2.06 = 94p.
  4. A jug holds 2 litres of juice. Each glass holds 125 ml. How many full glasses can be poured?
    Answer: 16 glasses
    Convert: 2 litres = 2,000 ml.  2,000 ÷ 125 = 16.
  5. A square has a perimeter of 52 cm. What is its area?
    Answer: 169 cm²
    Side = 52 ÷ 4 = 13 cm.  Area = 13 × 13 = 169 cm².

English

~20–25 minutes
Part 1 — SPaG & Vocabulary
  1. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?
    A) “I’m not sure,” said Ella, “but I think it’s this way.”
    B) “I’m not sure” said Ella “but I think it’s this way.”
    C) “I’m not sure,” said Ella “but I think it’s this way.”
    Answer: A
    Punctuation always goes inside the speech marks. When speech is split by a reporting clause (“said Ella”), a comma follows that clause and the second part of speech starts with a lower-case letter.
    Memory trick: RAPS — Reporting clause, comma After Punctuation inside Speech marks.
  2. Add a prefix to make the opposite of each word:
    (i) happy    (ii) visible    (iii) possible
    (i) unhappy    (ii) invisible    (iii) impossible
    “im-” is used before roots starting with p, b, or m (impatient, imbalance, immature). All others usually take “in-”.
  3. Identify the preposition in this sentence:
    The cat hid underneath the wooden table.
    Answer: underneath
    A preposition shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word — here it tells us where the cat hid. Other common prepositions: above, below, beside, through, across.
  4. What literary device is used in this sentence?
    “The silver snake of the river slid through the valley.”
    Primary answer: metaphor (the river is described as a snake, without “like” or “as”).
    Bonus: alliteration — the repeated “s” sound in silver, snake, slid gives the sentence a slithering, snake-like feel. Both are excellent to mention in an exam answer.
  5. Choose the correct word:
    The magician’s trick had a great _______ on the audience.
    A) affect    B) effect
    Answer: B) effect
    “Effect” is usually a noun (a result). “Affect” is usually a verb (to influence).
    Memory trick: RAVEN — Remember Affect is a Verb, Effect is a Noun.
Part 2 — Comprehension

Read this passage carefully, then answer the questions:

The market was alive. Voices, colours, and smells tumbled over each other in a cheerful chaos. Layla moved slowly through the crowd, her basket already heavy with tomatoes, herbs, and a loaf of bread still warm from the bakery. At the flower stall, she paused. An old woman behind the counter watched her with bright, patient eyes. “Take your time, child,” she said, in a voice as smooth as river stones. Layla chose a small bunch of yellow marigolds — the colour of the sun, the colour of her grandmother’s kitchen. She handed over her last coin and walked home with flowers in her arms and the smell of bread rising around her like a question she already knew the answer to.
  1. Name three items already in Layla’s basket before she reaches the flower stall.
    Answer: tomatoes, herbs, and a loaf of bread.
    All three are listed in the second sentence. The bread detail (“still warm from the bakery”) also tells us she has been shopping for a while.
  2. What does the word alive suggest about the market?
    Example answer: “Alive” is a metaphor suggesting the market is bursting with energy, as though it were a living creature. It creates a sense of movement and excitement before any specific detail is given.
  3. Find and copy a simile from the passage. What does it tell you about the old woman’s voice?
    Simile: “as smooth as river stones”
    It suggests her voice is calm, gentle, and worn smooth — like stones shaped by flowing water over a long time. It makes her seem kind and unhurried.
  4. Why do you think Layla chooses the yellow marigolds? Use evidence from the passage.
    Example answer: She chooses them because they remind her of her grandmother’s kitchen — “the colour of her grandmother’s kitchen.” This suggests the flowers carry a personal memory, making them emotionally significant rather than just decorative.
  5. What effect does the final sentence have on the reader? Comment on the language used.
    Example answer: The final sentence creates a feeling of warmth and belonging. The simile “like a question she already knew the answer to” suggests Layla feels a deep sense of certainty and comfort — home is familiar and safe. The long, flowing sentence also mirrors the gentle, unhurried pace of her walk home.
Part 3 — Writing Task
Prompt: You are walking through a busy market. Describe what you can see, hear, and smell. You notice something unusual at one of the stalls. What is it, and what do you do?

Write 8–10 sentences. Try to use:
  • A variety of sentence types — statements, questions, exclamations
  • At least one example of personification
  • Carefully chosen adjectives to build atmosphere
Example answer:
The market roared and sparkled around me. Stalls jostled for attention on either side of the narrow alley, their canopies flapping like bright flags in the breeze. The smell of roasted nuts drifted past, warm and sweet, followed immediately by the sharp bite of vinegar from the pickle cart. Everywhere voices called out, bantered, laughed. Then I saw it. Tucked between a scarf stall and a fruit seller was a small glass cabinet, no bigger than a shoebox, and inside it — glowing faintly blue — sat what appeared to be a tiny, beating heart. What on earth was it? I stepped closer, my curiosity pulling me forward like a magnet, and pressed my nose against the cold glass.

Check your own writing for:
• Sentence variety — question (“What on earth was it?”) and short punchy statement (“Then I saw it.”)
• Personification — “stalls jostled for attention”
• Sensory detail — sight, sound and smell all used

Bonus Maths Challenge

~5 minutes · optional
Harder Problems — Push Yourself!

These questions build on today’s topics. Show all working carefully.

  1. What is the smallest number that is a multiple of both 8 and 12? (This is called the Lowest Common Multiple, or LCM.)
    Answer: 24
    Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, 32, 40…
    Multiples of 12: 12, 24, 36…
    The first number on both lists is 24.
  2. Maya thinks of a number. She multiplies it by 5, adds 8, then divides by 3. The result is 11. What was her number?
    Answer: 5
    Work backwards:
    11 × 3 = 33  →  33 − 8 = 25  →  25 ÷ 5 = 5.
    Check: 5 × 5 = 25, 25 + 8 = 33, 33 ÷ 3 = 11. ✓
  3. A tap fills a 360-litre tank at a rate of 15 litres per minute. A drain removes water at 9 litres per minute. Both are running at the same time. How many minutes will it take to fill the tank?
    Answer: 60 minutes
    Net fill rate = 15 − 9 = 6 litres per minute.
    Time = 360 ÷ 6 = 60 minutes.

Word of the Day

~2 minutes
clamour noun / verb
KLAM-er
Definition: a loud, confused noise made by a large number of people or animals; to shout loudly and insistently as a crowd.
“Above the clamour of the market, Layla could just make out the flower-seller’s calm, unhurried voice.”
Your turn: Write a sentence using clamour in a setting other than a market — perhaps a school playground, a football stadium, or a parliament debate.

Tip: Clamour can replace overused words like “noise” or “shouting” in your writing. Examiners notice when you reach for a more precise or vivid word.
