📐 Maths (~20 minutes)

1. Core skills warm-up

  1. What is 11² + 12²?
    265. 11² = 121. 12² = 144. 121 + 144 = 265.
  2. What is 5/6 − 3/8?
    11/24. LCM of 6 and 8 = 24. 5/6 = 20/24. 3/8 = 9/24. 20/24 − 9/24 = 11/24.
  3. A polygon has interior angles summing to 1,260°. How many sides does it have?
    9 sides. (n−2) × 180 = 1,260 → n−2 = 7 → n = 9.
  4. Solve: 3(x − 4) = 2x + 1.
    x = 13. Expand: 3x − 12 = 2x + 1. Subtract 2x: x − 12 = 1. x = 13.
  5. What is 8% of £4,500?
    £360. 1% = £45. 8 × £45 = £360.

2. Word problems & exam-style practice

  1. A bus journey has 3 stops and takes 1 h 15 min in total, with equal time between stops. How long is each section?
    25 min each. 1 h 15 min = 75 min. 75 ÷ 3 = 25 min per section.
  2. A farmer has fields in ratio 5:3:2. Total area is 600 acres. What is the largest field?
    300 acres. Total parts = 10. One part = 60 acres. Largest = 5 × 60 = 300 acres.
  3. A cube has surface area 96 cm². What is the side length and volume?
    Side = 4 cm. Volume = 64 cm³. 6 faces: 96 ÷ 6 = 16 cm² per face. Side = √16 = 4. Volume = 4³ = 64.
  4. A train departs at 09:47 and arrives at 13:22. How long is the journey?
    3 hours 35 minutes. 09:47 → 10:00 = 13 min. 10:00 → 13:00 = 3 h. 13:00 → 13:22 = 22 min. Total: 3 h 35 min.
  5. Plot y = 2x − 1 for x = −1, 0, 1, 2, 3. State the coordinates.
    (−1, −3), (0, −1), (1, 1), (2, 3), (3, 5). Gradient = 2, y-intercept = −1.

📖 English (~20 minutes)

1. SPaG & vocabulary

  1. Explain the difference between concrete and abstract nouns in terms of a writer's choices.
    Concrete nouns name physical things ("chair", "blood") — vivid and immediate. Abstract nouns name ideas or emotions ("grief", "freedom") — important for themes but less sensory. Strong writers balance both: "She felt grief" (abstract) vs "She sat very still, staring at his empty chair" (concrete showing grief).
  2. What is prolepsis (flash-forward/foreshadowing)? Give an example.
    When a writer hints at or describes events yet to happen. Example: "I did not know, as I shook his hand that morning, that I would never see him again." Creates dramatic irony and tension.
  3. Punctuate this correctly: however the results which had been expected to show improvement were disappointing although the team remained optimistic
    However, the results, which had been expected to show improvement, were disappointing, although the team remained optimistic.
  4. What does magnanimous mean? Use it in a sentence.
    Generous in spirit, especially to a rival; noble and forgiving. Example: "Despite losing the final, she was magnanimous in defeat, congratulating the winners warmly."
  5. What is iambic pentameter? Mark the stresses in: Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
    Iambic pentameter = five iambs (da-DUM) per line = 10 syllables. Stresses: shall-I / com-PARE / thee-TO / a-SUM / mer's-DAY. Used throughout Shakespeare's sonnets and plays.

2. Comprehension — Words Without Borders

There is a word in German — Waldeinsamkeit — which means the feeling of solitude and connection that comes from being alone in the forest. English has no equivalent. It is not quite loneliness, because loneliness implies lack; it is not quite peace, because peace is passive. Waldeinsamkeit is active: you walk, you listen, the trees close around you, and you feel, simultaneously, very small and very present. Many languages have words for states of mind that English cannot quite capture: the Japanese mono no aware, the sense of the bittersweet beauty of impermanence; the Portuguese saudade, a deep emotional longing for something or someone that may be gone forever. The absence of a word does not mean the absence of the feeling. It means only that nobody, in that language, needed a word for it yet.
  1. What does Waldeinsamkeit mean?
    The feeling of solitude and connection from being alone in the forest — neither loneliness (which implies lack) nor passive peace, but an active state of feeling small and very present.
  2. How does the writer distinguish Waldeinsamkeit from loneliness and peace?
    Loneliness implies lack/absence of something desired; peace is passive. Waldeinsamkeit is active — you walk, listen, feel both small and present simultaneously.
  3. What other untranslatable words does the writer give?
    Japanese "mono no aware": the bittersweet beauty of impermanence. Portuguese "saudade": deep longing for something or someone that may be gone.
  4. What does "the absence of a word does not mean the absence of the feeling" mean?
    The feeling exists regardless of whether a language has named it. The lack of a word means that culture hasn't needed to label it yet — not that the experience is absent.
  5. What is the tone of this passage and how is it achieved?
    Thoughtful, philosophical, and gently celebratory of the diversity of human experience. Achieved through careful definition-building, listing cross-cultural examples, and the reflective final sentence.

3. Writing task

Invent your own untranslatable word for a feeling English doesn't have a word for. Define it carefully, give an example of when you might feel it, and explain why it needs a word.

There should be a word for the particular feeling when you return to a place you loved as a child and find it smaller than you remembered. Not disappointment exactly — the place hasn't changed. You have. Call it membrosis: the moment you realise a memory was larger than its origin. You feel it standing in a childhood bedroom now used as a study, or at the foot of a hill you once thought was a mountain. The world has not shrunk. Only your astonishment has. Membrosis is not sad; it is honest. It is the feeling of understanding, at last, how big you have become — and what you have had to give up to get there.

⭐ Bonus Maths Challenge

  1. Linear equations: A line passes through (2, 3) and (6, 11). Find the equation of the line in the form y = mx + c.
    y = 2x − 1. Gradient = (11−3)/(6−2) = 8/4 = 2. Using (2, 3): 3 = 2(2) + c → c = −1. Equation: y = 2x − 1.
  2. Fractions & time: A task takes 2¾ hours. If 3/5 of it has been completed, how many minutes remain?
    66 minutes. 2¾ hours = 165 min. Completed: 3/5 × 165 = 99 min. Remaining: 165 − 99 = 66 min.
  3. Polygon angles: The interior angle of a regular polygon is 150°. How many sides does it have?
    12 sides. Exterior angle = 180 − 150 = 30°. Number of sides = 360° ÷ 30° = 12.

💬 Word of the Day

contemplative
adjective
Expressing or involving prolonged thought; given to deep, quiet reflection. From Latin contemplari — to observe.
"Walking alone in the forest, she fell into a contemplative silence, aware of nothing but her own footsteps and the sound of the wind."
