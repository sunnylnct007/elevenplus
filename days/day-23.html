📐 Maths (~20 minutes)

1. Core skills warm-up

  1. Solve: 2x/5 − 1 = 3.
    x = 10. Add 1: 2x/5 = 4. Multiply by 5: 2x = 20. x = 10.
  2. What is 4/7 of 42?
    24. 42 ÷ 7 = 6. 6 × 4 = 24.
  3. Write the prime factorisation of 180.
    2² × 3² × 5. 180 = 2 × 90 = 2 × 2 × 45 = 4 × 9 × 5.
  4. Two angles on a straight line are in ratio 1:4. Find both angles.
    36° and 144°. Angles on a line sum to 180°. 1 part = 36°. 4 parts = 144°.
  5. What is 2.5% of £8,000?
    £200. 1% = £80. 2.5 × £80 = £200.

2. Word problems & exam-style practice

  1. A map has scale 1:25,000. A road is 8 cm on the map. What is the actual length in km?
    2 km. 8 × 25,000 = 200,000 cm = 2,000 m = 2 km.
  2. A card is 30 cm × 20 cm. Four 5 cm squares are cut from the corners, then the card is folded into an open box. Find the dimensions and volume.
    Box: 20 cm × 10 cm × 5 cm. Volume = 20 × 10 × 5 = 1,000 cm³.
  3. In a survey: 60% like chocolate, 25% like vanilla. Out of 200 people, how many like strawberry?
    30. Strawberry = 100 − 60 − 25 = 15%. 15% × 200 = 30.
  4. Prove algebraically that the product of two consecutive odd integers is always odd.
    Let them be (2n+1) and (2n+3). Product = (2n+1)(2n+3) = 4n²+8n+3 = 2(2n²+4n+1)+1. This has the form 2k+1 (an odd number). ✓
  5. A cylinder has the same height as its diameter. Its volume is 250π cm³. Find its radius.
    r = 5 cm. Height = diameter = 2r. V = πr²(2r) = 2πr³ = 250π → r³ = 125 → r = 5.

📖 English (~20 minutes)

1. SPaG & vocabulary

  1. What is a parenthetical clause? Give three ways to punctuate one.
    A clause that adds extra information which could be removed without changing the main meaning. Punctuation options: (1) Commas: "The house, which had been empty for years, was sold." (2) Dashes: "The house — which had been empty for years — was sold." (3) Brackets: "The house (which had been empty for years) was sold."
  2. What is the subjunctive mood? Give two examples.
    The subjunctive expresses wishes, hypotheticals, or conditions contrary to fact — often using "were" instead of "was." Examples: "If I were you, I would reconsider." "She demanded that he leave immediately." More common in formal or literary writing.
  3. Identify the literary technique and explain its effect: "The flames clawed at the curtains, hungry for more."
    Personification — "clawed" (an animalistic action) and "hungry" give the fire predatory, purposeful qualities. Effect: makes the fire seem malevolent and dangerous, intensifying the drama.
  4. What does incandescent mean? Give a literal and a figurative use.
    Literally: glowing with intense heat or light. Figuratively: intensely angry. Literal: "The incandescent bulb filled the room with warm light." Figurative: "She was incandescent with fury when she heard the news."
  5. What is free verse? How does it differ from blank verse?
    Free verse has no fixed metre or rhyme scheme — the poet makes deliberate rhythmic choices but is unconstrained. Blank verse has no rhyme but DOES have regular metre — usually iambic pentameter (10 syllables, da-DUM pattern). Shakespeare's plays use blank verse; much modern poetry uses free verse.

2. Comprehension — The Library

Nobody talks about the smell of libraries. They talk about the quiet, the long tables, the particular quality of the light through high windows — but not the smell, which is the best thing. It is the smell of paper and time, of dust that has settled on ideas, of thousands of different people's attention held in one place. I have been going to this library since I was seven years old. It is where I went when home was difficult and school was loud and my own head was too crowded to be in. I never borrowed anything extraordinary. But every time I walked in, I became slightly more myself. That is not a small thing.
  1. Why, according to the writer, do people overlook the smell of libraries?
    They focus on other qualities — the quiet, the light, the long tables — and overlook the smell, which the writer considers "the best thing."
  2. What is the smell of libraries compared to, and why is this effective?
    "Paper and time", "dust that has settled on ideas", "thousands of different people's attention." These descriptions make the smell intellectual and human — the residue of thought, not mere mustiness.
  3. Why did the writer go to the library?
    When home was difficult, school was loud, and their own head was too crowded — the library was a retreat and refuge.
  4. What does "every time I walked in, I became slightly more myself" mean?
    The library allowed the writer to recover a sense of identity — calm, undistracted, centred. The word "slightly" is precise and honest, avoiding overstatement.
  5. How does the final sentence function structurally and emotionally?
    "That is not a small thing." is short and declarative — it follows the modest admission ("slightly more myself") and defends it against underestimation. Its brevity gives it finality and quiet force.

3. Writing task

Write a short piece about a place that has meant something to you — a refuge or space where you felt more yourself. Use: specific sensory detail (not just visual), a clear sense of why you went there, and a modest final line that understates the importance of the place.

The school pond was small enough to jump across and smelled of mud and something alive, though you could never see quite what. Nobody went there at lunchtime except me. I went because it was the only part of school that didn't want anything from you — it wasn't checking if you had done your homework or asking you to sit still. The water was always moving slightly, even when there was no wind. I liked that. I would sit on an old pallet someone had left as a bench and do nothing in particular, and after ten minutes I was always better. I never told anyone about it. Some things work only because they belong to you alone.

⭐ Bonus Maths Challenge

  1. Cylinders: A cylinder has radius 4 cm and height 10 cm. Find its volume and curved surface area. (π = 3.14)
    Volume = π × 16 × 10 = 502.4 cm³. Curved SA = 2πrh = 2 × 3.14 × 4 × 10 = 251.2 cm².
  2. Proof: Show that the sum of any two consecutive odd numbers is divisible by 4.
    Let consecutive odd numbers be (2n−1) and (2n+1). Sum = 4n. Since 4n is divisible by 4 for all integers n, the sum is always divisible by 4. ✓
  3. Scale: A map has scale 1:50,000. Two towns are 6.4 cm apart on the map. What is the real distance in km? How many cm on the map represents 5 km?
    Real distance: 6.4 × 50,000 = 320,000 cm = 3.2 km. For 5 km: 5 km = 500,000 cm. 500,000 ÷ 50,000 = 10 cm on the map.

💬 Word of the Day

solace
noun / verb
Comfort or consolation in a time of distress or sadness. From Latin solacium — comfort.
"For years, the library had been her solace — a place to think clearly when the rest of life felt too loud."
