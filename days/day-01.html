Day 1

Sutton Grammar 11+ Daily Practice

Maths & English — about 40–45 minutes total

Maths ~20 min English ~20–25 min Try every question on your own first!
🧮

Maths

~20 minutes
Part 1 — Core Skills Warm-up

Answer these quickly — mentally or with brief working:

  1. What is 7 × 8?
    Answer: 56
    Think of 7 groups of 8: 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 = 56.
  2. What is 54 ÷ 6?
    Answer: 9
    Because 6 × 9 = 54, so 54 divided by 6 is 9.
  3. Round 3,468 to the nearest hundred.
    Answer: 3,500
    The hundreds digit is 4; the tens digit is 6 (≥5), so round up: 3,500.
  4. Find ¼ of 32.
    Answer: 8
    One quarter means divide by 4: 32 ÷ 4 = 8.
  5. Find 10% of 250.
    Answer: 25
    10% = one tenth, so 250 ÷ 10 = 25.
Part 2 — Word Problems & Exam-Style Practice

Show clear working for each question:

  1. A box holds 24 apples. Sam buys 3 boxes. How many apples does he have in total?
    Answer: 72 apples
    Apples per box × boxes: 24 × 3 = 72.
  2. There are 48 sweets. ¾ of them are cola flavour. How many cola sweets are there?
    Answer: 36 cola sweets
    Find ¼: 48 ÷ 4 = 12.  Then ¾ = 3 × 12 = 36.
  3. A book costs £6.50. Amir buys 4 books. How much does he pay altogether?
    Answer: £26.00
    6.50 × 4 = (6 × 4) + (0.50 × 4) = 24 + 2 = £26.
  4. A train journey starts at 09:35 and ends at 11:10. How long does the journey take?
    Answer: 1 hour 35 minutes
    09:35 → 10:35 = 1 hour; 10:35 → 11:10 = 35 minutes. Total: 1h 35m.
  5. In a class of 30 children, 40% are left-handed. How many children are left-handed?
    Answer: 12 children
    10% of 30 = 3, so 40% = 4 × 3 = 12.

English

~20–25 minutes
Part 1 — SPaG & Vocabulary
  1. Choose the correct spelling:
    A) recieve    B) receive    C) receeve
    Answer: B) receive
    Use “i before e except after c” → r e c e i v e.
  2. Add the missing punctuation:
    on saturday james went to the park he met his friend ben there
    Corrected: On Saturday, James went to the park. He met his friend Ben there.
    Capital letters for sentence starts and proper nouns; full stop between sentences; comma after “Saturday”.
  3. Identify the verb in:
    The cat chased the mouse across the garden.
    Answer: chased
    The verb is the doing/action word in the sentence.
  4. Choose a synonym for happy:
    A) joyful    B) silent    C) worried
    Answer: A) joyful
    A synonym has a similar meaning. Joyful ≈ happy.
  5. Choose an antonym for gentle:
    A) soft    B) calm    C) rough
    Answer: C) rough
    An antonym has the opposite meaning. Rough ≠ gentle.
Part 2 — Comprehension

Read this passage carefully, then answer the questions:

Ben stared out of the classroom window. Dark clouds were gathering over the playground, and the wind was beginning to shake the trees. The teacher continued to talk about fractions, but Ben was thinking about the football match after school. If the rain grew heavier, the match would be cancelled, and he had been looking forward to it all week.
  1. What lesson is the teacher talking about?
    Answer: Fractions (Maths)
    The passage states: “the teacher continued to talk about fractions.”
  2. Where is Ben sitting?
    Answer: In the classroom
    He is looking out of the classroom window.
  3. Why might the football match be cancelled?
    Answer: Because of heavy rain.
    The text says: “If the rain grew heavier, the match would be cancelled.”
  4. Find and copy a phrase that shows the weather is getting worse.
    Example: “Dark clouds were gathering”
    Other acceptable answers: “the wind was beginning to shake the trees”; “if the rain grew heavier”.
  5. How do you know that Ben cares about the football match? Explain in one sentence.
    Example answer: He has been looking forward to the match all week and is worried it will be cancelled, showing it is very important to him.
Part 3 — Writing Task
Prompt: You arrive at the football pitch and see that the weather has changed again. Describe what you see, how you feel, and what happens next.

Write 8–10 sentences. Try to use:
  • Full stops and commas correctly
  • At least three interesting adjectives
  • One example of speech (with speech marks)
Example answer:
The football pitch was almost unrecognisable. Puddles covered the muddy grass and the bitter wind howled around the empty stands. I shivered as cold rain splashed my face. “Are we still playing?” I shouted to my coach. He smiled and nodded, so I pulled up my soggy socks and ran onto the pitch, feeling nervously excited.

Check your own writing for: capital letters and full stops, commas in the right places, interesting adjectives (describe the pitch and weather), speech marks around spoken words.
